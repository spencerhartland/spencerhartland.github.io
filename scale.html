Smart Coffee Scale

I love coffee! My typical day can include 3+ cups! I prefer espresso to filter coffee, and my drink of choice is an oat milk latte. Keeping in mind my habit for multiple cups a day... relying on coffee shops gets expensive. As such, I'm planning to buy an expresso machine!

Of course, I have to be nerdy about my coffee so I really wanted a fancy coffee scale like the Acaia Pyxis or Lunar, but I really don't want to pay for one. That's what lead me to this project: a smart coffee scale powered by a Raspberry Pi Pico W.

Components

  • Raspberry Pi Pico W
  • 128x32 monochrome OLED
  • HX711 ADC / load cell amplifier
  • Load cell
  • 500 mAh Li-ion battery
  • Pimoroni Pico LiPo Shim

Overview

So the goal for this project was to make a scale that emulated, as closely as possible, the Acaia Pyxis. The Pyxis is TINY: 65mm x 65mm x 13 mm. Designing a scale built from off-the-shelf components that fit into those dimensions was the biggest hurdle. Originally, the load cell I was using was slightly too large and forced me to increase my scale's dimensions to about 75 mm x 75mm x 15 mm. Fortunately, I managed to salvage a much smaller load cell from a kitchen scale I already owned! This allowed my design to meet the original goal of matching the Pyxis' dimensions.

As of now, the hardware is complete. Software is the easy part, so that should be coming soon (definitely before I buy my espresso machine!).
